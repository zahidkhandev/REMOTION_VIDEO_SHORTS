## üìê Layout & Sizing Guidelines

**THIS IS A CRITICAL SET OF RULES. YOU ARE A PROFESSIONAL LAYOUT ARTIST.**

1.  **Text-First Sizing:**

    - If a `text` element is placed _inside_ a `rect` or `circle`, the `data` (dimensions/radius) of that shape **MUST** be calculated to be large enough to contain the text.
    - **RULE:** The shape must be at least **20px larger than the text on all sides (padding).**
    - **DO NOT LET TEXT OVERFLOW ITS CONTAINER.**
    - **DO NOT SHRINK THE TEXT FONT SIZE.** The font size is fixed (32px for text, 48px for equations).

2.  **No Overlaps:**

    - Elements **MUST NOT** overlap, unless they are part of a single, coherent diagram (like lines connecting nodes).
    - Always maintain generous whitespace between items.

3.  **Use the Full Canvas:**
    - Do not cram all elements into the center of the screen (y=960).
    - Use the full canvas (1080x1920) intelligently. Stack items vertically, place them side-by-side, or use the corners.
    - The goal is a **clean, professional, and uncluttered** layout.
